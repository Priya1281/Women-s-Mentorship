The problem

Women aren't held back by capability. They're held back by the system.

Five structural and social barriers quietly shape who gets to grow — and who doesn't. This program is designed to dismantle every one of them.

01

Limited access to high-visibility opportunities

The stretch assignments, the high-stakes projects, the rooms where decisions are made — these don't reach everyone equally.

02

Lower sponsorship in decision-making forums

Mentors give advice. Sponsors open doors. Women are over-mentored and under-sponsored.

03

Reduced exposure to leadership networks

Access to the right people — and the right conversations — is rarely distributed fairly.

04

Social conditioning that silences ambition

Years of being told to be agreeable, not to ask for too much, and to wait your turn makes it genuinely hard to voice what you want — even when you know exactly what that is.

05

The assumption that life milestones are career interruptions

Women are still seen through the lens of what might pause them — a wedding, a pregnancy — rather than what they're actively building. This shapes who gets invested in, and who gets passed over.

High selectivity

Every participant enters with a defined goal.

This is not a community program or a celebratory initiative. Selection is application-based and deliberate. Each woman must articulate a clear, specific leadership goal she will work towards over the 90 days — and own it publicly at the closing showcase.

Clear stretch goalA defined leadership outcome you are working towards — not a vague aspiration.
Manager endorsementYour manager commits to creating space and championing your stretch goal.
Full program commitmentAll four modules, the offsite, and the closing showcase. No partial participation.
Cohort of 10–15Small by design. Every participant is expected to contribute, not just attend.

Program structure

90 days · 4 modules · 1 showcase.

Each module is led by a woman from a different walk of life — founders, operators, artists, scientists, leaders who've navigated their own version of this. Every module includes one group workshop and dedicated 1:1 time with another woman in the cohort.

Kick-off

Induction & goal-setting

Launch event, applications reviewed, cohort announced. Each participant defines and submits her 90-day stretch goal before Module 1 begins. You'll also meet your sponsor for the first time.

Weeks 1–3 · Module 01

Understanding the room

How power actually moves in organisations — who gets heard, who shapes decisions, and why it often isn't the most qualified person in the room. You'll map the informal power structures around you and figure out how to show up differently in them.

1 workshop 1:1 cohort session Led by a woman from a different field

Weeks 4–6 · Module 02

Making your work visible

How to get credit for what you do, speak up in rooms that matter, and translate the work you're already doing into language that lands with the people who need to hear it. Because good work that goes unseen doesn't move careers forward.

1 workshop 1:1 cohort session Led by a woman from a different field

Weeks 7–9 · Module 03

Learning to say no

Protecting your focus, your energy, and your ambitions — without guilt. This isn't about being difficult. It's about being deliberate. You'll practise the conversations that most women have been quietly avoiding, and learn that boundaries are a leadership skill.

1 workshop 1:1 cohort session Led by a woman from a different field

Weeks 10–11 · Module 04

Finding your people

Who opens doors for you — and how do you actually activate those relationships? This module is about identifying the five people who will shape your next decade, building your personal board of directors, and making your sponsor relationship work for you.

1 workshop 1:1 cohort session Led by a woman from a different field

Week 12

Closing showcase

Each participant presents her stretch outcome to leadership. This is the moment — not a debrief, a debut.

The core strategic lever

Internal sponsorship, not mentorship.

Mentorship provides advice

Guidance, perspective, shared experience. Reactive to your questions. Valuable — but not sufficient.

  • Offers counsel and experience
  • Reactive to your agenda
  • Develops your thinking
  • Stays within your current circles

Sponsorship creates opportunity

Each participant is paired 1:1 with a senior Plum leader who actively advocates — not just advises.

  • Visible advocacy in leadership forums
  • High-impact introductions and warm handoffs
  • Strategic navigation of the organisation
  • One door-opening action per cohort member

What we ask of sponsors

~3 hours total Across the full 90-day program — roughly one hour a month
Kick-off call 30 mins to meet your participant and understand her stretch goal
Midpoint check-in 45 mins at Week 6 to review progress and unblock where needed
One active act An introduction, a nomination, or a visibility opportunity — something real
Closing showcase Show up and be seen cheering her on
Speak up in rooms Name her when she's not in the room. That's what sponsorship looks like.

Community layer

Women community spaces — beyond the cohort.

Before the cohort closes in on itself, we open it outward. Brown bag lunches bring together women from across the PS team and Plum — different functions, different backgrounds, different stories.

Session 1 · Month 1

Brown bag lunch — PS team

Organised for the PS team. Open format: women from different functions and tenures share their experience at Plum. The cohort listens, asks, connects.

Session 2 · Month 2

Brown bag lunch — cross-function

A wider invite — women from across Plum with diverse career paths. The cohort facilitates, not just participates. Practice in action.

Month 3 onwards

Cohort-led community

From Month 3, the cohort owns it. They decide the format, the invite list, the agenda. The program hands them the keys — and they drive.

What we need from leadership

Three asks to make this work.

1

Sponsorship

Senior leaders commit to one participant each — advocate actively, make one meaningful introduction, and show up to the closing showcase.

2

Manager support

Managers endorse their participant's application, champion their stretch goal, and create real space for leadership growth — not just in theory.

3

Visibility

Create one opportunity per participant during the 90 days to present, lead, or influence at a meaningful forum. Not symbolic — consequential.
